# All 5e Backgrounds — Quick Reference

Source books noted in parentheses. PHB = Player's Handbook (always available). Others — ask your DM.

---

## Player's Handbook Backgrounds

### Acolyte (PHB)
- **Skills:** Insight, Religion
- **Tools/Languages:** 2 languages
- **Equipment:** Holy symbol, prayer book, 5 sticks of incense, vestments, 15 gp
- **Feature — Shelter of the Faithful:** Temples of your faith will house and feed you and your allies. You can call on priests for non-dangerous assistance.
- **Best For:** Clerics, Paladins, Monks, or any character with a religious order background. Insight is broadly useful; Religion is INT-based, so it pairs best with decent INT.

### Charlatan (PHB)
- **Skills:** Deception, Sleight of Hand
- **Tools:** Disguise kit, Forgery kit
- **Equipment:** Fine clothes, disguise kit, tools of your con, 15 gp
- **Feature — False Identity:** You have a second identity with documentation and established contacts. You can forge documents if you have examples.
- **Best For:** Rogues (especially Assassins and Masterminds), Bards, Warlocks, or any CHA/DEX character built around infiltration and deception. The false identity feature is exceptional for spy and con artist concepts. Note: Assassin Rogues get disguise kit proficiency at level 3, so check for overlap.

### Criminal / Spy (PHB)
- **Skills:** Deception, Stealth
- **Tools:** Gaming set (one of your choice), Thieves' tools
- **Equipment:** Crowbar, dark common clothes with hood, 15 gp
- **Feature — Criminal Contact:** You have a reliable contact who acts as a liaison to a network of criminals. You can send and receive messages through them.
- **Best For:** Rogues, Rangers, or any character with an underworld or espionage background. The **Spy variant** uses the same mechanics but reframes you as an intelligence operative. Note: Thieves' tools overlap with Rogue class proficiency — ask your DM about swapping the duplicate.

### Entertainer (PHB)
- **Skills:** Acrobatics, Performance
- **Tools:** Disguise kit, 1 musical instrument
- **Equipment:** Musical instrument, a favor from an admirer, costume, 15 gp
- **Feature — By Popular Demand:** You can find a place to perform for free lodging and food, and strangers recognize you.
- **Best For:** Bards, Monks (the acrobatic performer angle), or any CHA-based character. Performance is CHA-based, so low-CHA characters will struggle with half this background.

### Folk Hero (PHB)
- **Skills:** Animal Handling, Survival
- **Tools:** Artisan's tools (one of your choice), Vehicles (land)
- **Equipment:** Artisan's tools, shovel, iron pot, common clothes, 10 gp
- **Feature — Rustic Hospitality:** Common folk will shelter you and shield you from the law (but not risk their lives).
- **Best For:** Rangers, Druids, Barbarians, or any WIS/STR character from humble origins. Both skills are practical for wilderness campaigns.

### Guild Artisan / Guild Merchant (PHB)
- **Skills:** Insight, Persuasion
- **Tools:** Artisan's tools (one of your choice)
- **Languages:** 1 language
- **Equipment:** Artisan's tools, letter of introduction from guild, traveler's clothes, 15 gp
- **Feature — Guild Membership:** Fellow guild members will house you, pay for your funeral, and support you in legal disputes. You have access to a guild hall for meetings and contacts.
- **Best For:** CHA/WIS characters — Bards, Clerics, Paladins, or Rogues built for social encounters. The guild network is useful in urban campaigns.

### Hermit (PHB)
- **Skills:** Medicine, Religion
- **Tools:** Herbalism kit
- **Languages:** 1 language
- **Equipment:** Scroll case with notes, winter blanket, common clothes, herbalism kit, 5 gp
- **Feature — Discovery:** You have made a unique and powerful discovery — a truth, a site, a fact, or something else. Work with your DM to define it.
- **Best For:** Clerics, Druids, Monks, or Wizards with a reclusive backstory. The Discovery feature is a great plot hook. Medicine and Religion are niche but fit the theme.

### Noble / Knight (PHB)
- **Skills:** History, Persuasion
- **Tools:** Gaming set (one of your choice)
- **Languages:** 1 language
- **Equipment:** Fine clothes, signet ring, scroll of pedigree, 25 gp
- **Feature — Position of Privilege:** Common folk do their best to accommodate you. You can secure an audience with local nobility.
- **Best For:** Paladins, Fighters, Bards, or any CHA character of high social standing. The Knight variant suits martial characters from military orders. History is INT-based, so pair with decent INT.

### Outlander (PHB)
- **Skills:** Athletics, Survival
- **Tools:** 1 musical instrument
- **Languages:** 1 language
- **Equipment:** Staff, hunting trap, animal trophy, traveler's clothes, 10 gp
- **Feature — Wanderer:** You have excellent memory for geography and can always recall the general layout of terrain. You can find food and water for up to 6 people daily in the wild.
- **Best For:** Rangers, Barbarians, Druids, or STR/WIS characters from the wilderness. The auto-foraging feature is excellent in travel-heavy campaigns.

### Sage (PHB)
- **Skills:** Arcana, History
- **Tools:** None
- **Languages:** 2 languages
- **Equipment:** Bottle of ink, quill, small knife, letter from dead colleague, common clothes, 10 gp
- **Feature — Researcher:** When you don't know information, you often know where and from whom to find it.
- **Best For:** Wizards, Artificers, or any INT-based character. Both skills are INT-dependent. The Researcher feature shines in investigation and lore-heavy campaigns.

### Sailor / Pirate (PHB)
- **Skills:** Athletics, Perception
- **Tools:** Navigator's tools, Vehicles (water)
- **Equipment:** Belaying pin (club), 50 ft. silk rope, common clothes, 10 gp
- **Feature — Ship's Passage:** You can secure free passage on a ship for you and your party in exchange for crew work.
- **Best For:** STR/WIS characters, Fighters, or Barbarians with a nautical backstory. Perception is universally useful; Athletics requires decent STR. The Pirate variant gives a more intimidating reputation.

### Soldier (PHB)
- **Skills:** Athletics, Intimidation
- **Tools:** Gaming set (one of your choice), Vehicles (land)
- **Equipment:** Insignia of rank, trophy from a fallen enemy, bone dice or cards, common clothes, 10 gp
- **Feature — Military Rank:** Soldiers of your former affiliation recognize your rank and defer to you. You can pull rank to gain access to military resources and camps.
- **Best For:** Fighters, Paladins, Barbarians, or any STR/CHA character with military history. Athletic + Intimidation is a classic bruiser combination.

### Urchin (PHB)
- **Skills:** Sleight of Hand, Stealth
- **Tools:** Disguise kit, Thieves' tools
- **Equipment:** Small knife, map of your home city, pet mouse, token from parents, common clothes, 10 gp
- **Feature — City Secrets:** You know the secret patterns and shortcuts of urban areas. You can move through cities at double speed when not in combat.
- **Best For:** Rogues, Monks, or any DEX character with a street upbringing. Both skills are DEX-based and excellent. City Secrets is mechanically strong for urban campaigns. Note: Thieves' tools overlap with Rogue class proficiency.

## Sword Coast Adventurer's Guide (SCAG)

### City Watch / Investigator (SCAG)
- **Skills:** Athletics, Insight
- **Tools:** None
- **Languages:** 2 languages
- **Equipment:** Uniform, horn, manacles, 10 gp
- **Feature — Watcher's Eye:** You can find the local watch or similar organization in a settlement, and you can find local criminal dens.
- **Best For:** Fighters, Paladins, or Clerics with a law-enforcement backstory. The Investigator variant suits INT/WIS characters solving crimes. Insight is broadly useful; Athletics needs STR.

### Clan Crafter (SCAG)
- **Skills:** History, Insight
- **Tools:** Artisan's tools (one of your choice)
- **Languages:** 1 (Dwarvish or another)
- **Equipment:** Artisan's tools, maker's mark chisel, traveler's clothes, gem worth 10 gp, 5 gp
- **Feature — Respect of the Stout Folk:** Dwarves grant you free room and board (modest).
- **Best For:** Dwarven characters of any class, Artificers, or Fighters/Clerics with a crafting background. History is INT-based; Insight is WIS-based.

### Cloistered Scholar (SCAG)
- **Skills:** History, plus one of Arcana, Nature, or Religion
- **Tools:** None
- **Languages:** 2 languages
- **Equipment:** Scholar's robes, writing kit, borrowed book, 10 gp
- **Feature — Library Access:** You have access to libraries and archives in academic institutions.
- **Best For:** Wizards, Artificers, or any INT-based character. All skill options are INT-dependent, so this background is weak for low-INT characters.

### Courtier (SCAG)
- **Skills:** Insight, Persuasion
- **Tools:** None
- **Languages:** 2 languages
- **Equipment:** Fine clothes, 5 gp
- **Feature — Court Functionary:** You understand the bureaucracy and etiquette of noble courts. You can navigate political structures to find the person you need to talk to.
- **Best For:** Bards, Paladins, Warlocks, or any CHA/WIS character involved in political intrigue. The courtier-as-spy angle works well for infiltrators with decent CHA.

### Faction Agent (SCAG)
- **Skills:** Insight, plus one of your choice from: Animal Handling, Arcana, Deception, History, Intimidation, Investigation, Medicine, Nature, Perception, Performance, Persuasion, Religion, Survival
- **Tools:** None
- **Languages:** 2 languages
- **Equipment:** Faction badge/emblem, copy of a seminal faction text, common clothes, 15 gp
- **Feature — Safe Haven:** You have access to a secret network of contacts and safe houses associated with your faction. You can receive assistance and information from fellow agents.
- **Best For:** Any class — this is one of the most flexible backgrounds in the game. Insight is universally useful, and you pick your second skill freely. The safe house network is powerful for any character tied to an organization (Harpers, Zhentarim, Order of the Gauntlet, etc.). Excellent for Rogues, Rangers, and anyone with a WIS-based build.

### Far Traveler (SCAG)
- **Skills:** Insight, Perception
- **Tools:** 1 musical instrument or 1 gaming set
- **Languages:** 1 language
- **Equipment:** Traveler's clothes, 1 musical instrument or gaming set, maps from homeland, piece of jewelry worth 10 gp, 5 gp
- **Feature — All Eyes on You:** Your appearance and mannerisms mark you as foreign. People are curious about you and will want to hear your stories. You can leverage this interest to gain access to people and places.
- **Best For:** Any WIS-based character — Rogues, Rangers, Monks, Clerics, Druids. Both skills are WIS-based and among the most commonly rolled in the game. The feature naturally draws NPCs to you, which is great for quiet characters who wouldn't normally initiate social encounters. Perfect for any character from a distant land.

### Inheritor (SCAG)
- **Skills:** Survival, plus one of Arcana, History, or Religion
- **Tools:** Gaming set (one of your choice)
- **Languages:** 1 language
- **Equipment:** Inheritance item (define it), traveler's clothes, 15 gp
- **Feature — Inheritance:** You have inherited something of great value — not necessarily monetary. Work with DM to define it.
- **Best For:** Any character whose backstory centers on a meaningful object or legacy. Survival is WIS-based; the second skill is INT-based. Best for WIS/INT characters. The inheritance is a great plot hook.

### Knight of the Order (SCAG)
- **Skills:** Persuasion, plus one of Arcana, History, Nature, or Religion
- **Tools:** Gaming set or musical instrument
- **Languages:** 1 language
- **Equipment:** Traveler's clothes, signet/banner of rank, 10 gp
- **Feature — Knightly Regard:** Members of your order and their allies provide shelter and assistance.
- **Best For:** Paladins, Fighters, Clerics, or CHA characters affiliated with a knightly or religious order. Persuasion requires decent CHA.

### Mercenary Veteran (SCAG)
- **Skills:** Athletics, Persuasion
- **Tools:** Gaming set (one of your choice), Vehicles (land)
- **Equipment:** Uniform, insignia of rank, gaming set, 10 gp
- **Feature — Mercenary Life:** You know how to find mercenary work and mercenary companies in a region.
- **Best For:** Fighters, Barbarians, or STR/CHA characters. Both skills require strong physical or social stats. Similar to Soldier but with a sellsword flavor.

### Urban Bounty Hunter (SCAG)
- **Skills:** Choose 2 from Deception, Insight, Persuasion, Stealth
- **Tools:** Choose 2 from gaming set, musical instrument, Thieves' tools
- **Equipment:** Appropriate clothes, 20 gp
- **Feature — Ear to the Ground:** You have contacts in every city who provide information about people and their movements.
- **Best For:** Rogues, Rangers, Fighters, or any character who hunts targets in cities. Extremely flexible — pick skills that match your ability scores. The intelligence network feature is strong for information-gathering campaigns. Note: Thieves' tools may overlap with Rogue class proficiency.

### Uthgardt Tribe Member (SCAG)
- **Skills:** Athletics, Survival
- **Tools:** Artisan's tools or musical instrument
- **Languages:** 1 language
- **Feature:** Barbarian tribal hospitality.
- **Best For:** Barbarians or STR/WIS wilderness characters from the Sword Coast region.

### Waterdhavian Noble (SCAG)
- **Skills:** History, Persuasion
- **Tools:** Gaming set or musical instrument
- **Languages:** 1 language
- **Feature:** Access to Waterdeep's noble circles.
- **Best For:** CHA/INT characters in Waterdeep-based campaigns. Very setting-specific.

## Other Sourcebook Backgrounds

### Anthropologist (Tomb of Annihilation)
- **Skills:** Insight, Religion
- **Tools:** None
- **Languages:** 2 languages
- **Feature — Adept Linguist:** You can communicate basic ideas with creatures that don't share your language after observing them for a day.
- **Best For:** WIS characters in exploration-heavy campaigns. Insight is broadly useful; Religion is INT-based. The language feature is unique and thematic for characters who study foreign cultures.

### Archaeologist (Tomb of Annihilation)
- **Skills:** History, Survival
- **Tools:** Cartographer's tools or Navigator's tools
- **Languages:** 1 language
- **Feature — Historical Knowledge:** When entering a ruin or dungeon, you can determine its original purpose and builders.
- **Best For:** INT/WIS characters in dungeon-crawling campaigns. History is INT-based; Survival is WIS-based. The dungeon knowledge feature is niche but flavorful.

### Haunted One (Curse of Strahd)
- **Skills:** Choose 2 from Arcana, Investigation, Religion, Survival
- **Tools:** None
- **Languages:** 1 exotic language (Abyssal, Celestial, Deep Speech, Draconic, Infernal, Primordial, Sylvan, Undercommon)
- **Equipment:** Monster hunter's pack, common clothes, trinket of special significance, 1 sp
- **Feature — Heart of Darkness:** Common folk sense something about you and will go out of their way to help you — offering shelter, food, and aid — though they fear you.
- **Best For:** Any class in a horror or gothic campaign. Flexible skill choices let you match your ability scores. The "people fear you" feature is great flavor for dark or brooding characters. Investigation + Survival is a solid combo for INT/WIS characters.

### Feylost (Wild Beyond the Witchlight)
- **Skills:** Deception, Survival
- **Tools:** 1 musical instrument
- **Languages:** 1 Fey language (Elvish, Gnomish, Goblin, or Sylvan)
- **Feature — Feywild Connection:** Fey creatures find you curious and may offer small gifts or assistance.
- **Best For:** CHA/WIS characters in Feywild-themed campaigns. Druids, Rangers, Bards, or Warlocks with fey connections.

### Witchlight Hand (Wild Beyond the Witchlight)
- **Skills:** Performance, Sleight of Hand
- **Tools:** Disguise kit or 1 musical instrument
- **Languages:** None
- **Feature:** Carnival connections.
- **Best For:** Bards, Rogues, or CHA/DEX characters with a carnival or circus background. Performance is CHA-based.

## Background Selection Guide

Choosing a background is about matching **skills to your best ability scores** and finding a **feature that fits your story**. Here's a quick guide by character archetype:

| Character Archetype | Top Backgrounds |
|---|---|
| **WIS-based scout** (Rogue, Ranger, Monk) | Far Traveler, Faction Agent, Urban Bounty Hunter |
| **CHA-based social character** (Bard, Warlock, Paladin) | Courtier, Noble, Guild Artisan, Charlatan |
| **DEX-based infiltrator** (Rogue, Monk) | Criminal (Spy), Charlatan, Urchin, Urban Bounty Hunter |
| **STR-based warrior** (Fighter, Barbarian, Paladin) | Soldier, Outlander, Sailor, Folk Hero |
| **INT-based scholar** (Wizard, Artificer) | Sage, Cloistered Scholar, Archaeologist |
| **Wilderness survivor** (Ranger, Druid, Barbarian) | Outlander, Folk Hero, Hermit |
| **Organization operative** (any class) | Faction Agent, Criminal (Spy), Knight of the Order |
