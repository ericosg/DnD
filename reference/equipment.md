# Equipment Reference — Weapons, Armor, Gear & Costs

---

## Weapon Properties — What the Keywords Mean

| Property | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Finesse** | You can use DEX instead of STR for attack and damage rolls. Essential for DEX-based builds (Rogues, Monks, DEX Fighters). |
| **Light** | Can be used for two-weapon fighting (dual wield). Both weapons must be light. |
| **Thrown** | Can be thrown for a ranged attack. Uses STR unless it also has Finesse (then DEX). The weapon leaves your hand. |
| **Ammunition** | Requires ammo (arrows, bolts). You can recover half your spent ammo after a fight. |
| **Two-Handed** | Requires both hands. Can't dual wield or hold a shield. |
| **Versatile** | Can be used one-handed or two-handed. Two-handed does more damage (listed as e.g., 1d8/1d10). |
| **Reach** | Melee range is 10 ft. instead of 5 ft. |
| **Heavy** | Small creatures have disadvantage. No effect on Medium or larger characters. |
| **Loading** | Can only fire once per action regardless of Extra Attack. Crossbow Expert feat removes this. |
| **Special** | Has unique rules. See the specific weapon. |

### Range Numbers (e.g., 20/60 ft.)
The first number is **normal range** — roll normally. The second is **long range** — you can reach it but attack with **disadvantage**. Beyond long range, you can't hit it.

**Armor Selection Guide:**
- **High DEX (14+), stealth matters:** Studded leather. Your DEX does all the work.
- **Medium DEX (12-14), stealth matters:** Breastplate or chain shirt.
- **Stealth doesn't matter, STR 15+:** Plate armor for maximum AC.
- **Stealth doesn't matter, STR 13+:** Chain mail (starting) into splint, then plate.

## How to Choose Your Starting Equipment

Most characters start with equipment from their **class** and **background**. If your DM offers the option to take starting gold instead and buy your own gear, here's a guide by character category.

### Martial Characters (Fighter, Barbarian, Paladin, Ranger)

**Priorities:**
1. **Best armor you're proficient in.** Heavy armor users: chain mail (16 AC) is usually free from class. Light/medium armor users: leather (11 + DEX) or scale mail (14 + DEX max 2), upgrading to studded leather or breastplate when affordable.
2. **Primary weapon matching your build.** STR builds: longsword/greataxe/greatsword/halberd. DEX builds: rapier (single weapon) or two shortswords (dual wield). Ranged: longbow or hand crossbow.
3. **A ranged backup.** Melee characters should carry javelins (STR) or a shortbow (DEX) for enemies you can't reach.
4. **A shield** if you're using a one-handed weapon and want AC over damage.
5. **An explorer's pack or dungeoneer's pack** for basic adventuring supplies.

### Full Casters (Wizard, Sorcerer, Warlock, Cleric, Druid, Bard)

**Priorities:**
1. **Spellcasting focus or component pouch.** Arcane focus (Wizard/Sorcerer/Warlock), holy symbol (Cleric/Paladin), druidic focus (Druid), or component pouch (works for anyone).
2. **Best armor you're proficient in.** Clerics and Druids get medium armor; Wizards and Sorcerers get nothing (Mage Armor spell helps). Bards get light armor.
3. **A backup weapon for when you're out of spell slots.** Cantrips are usually better, but a light crossbow or dagger doesn't hurt.
4. **A scholar's pack or explorer's pack.**
5. **A spell component that costs gold** if any of your starting spells require one (check your spell descriptions).

### Hybrid Characters (Rogue, Monk, Artificer, Eldritch Knight, Bladesinger)

**Priorities:**
1. **Weapons matching your ability scores.** DEX characters: rapier or dual shortswords, plus a shortbow. Monks: any simple weapon (shortsword works too) — your martial arts eventually outscale weapon dice.
2. **Light armor** (Rogues, Artificers) or **no armor** (Monks get Unarmored Defense). Upgrade to studded leather as soon as possible for light armor users.
3. **Thieves' tools** if you're a Rogue (you get them free from class).
4. **A burglar's pack** (Rogues) or dungeoneer's pack (most others).
5. **Utility items:** A few daggers for throwing, rope, a grappling hook (2 gp), and a healer's kit (5 gp) are all cheap and useful.
