# Races & Classes — Who Are You?

Two big choices define your character: **race** (what you are) and **class** (what you do). You've made these choices in BG3 — the options are largely the same, but let's walk through them with a tabletop lens.

---

## Part 1: Races

Your race determines your physical traits, some innate abilities, and (optionally) where you fit culturally in the world. In 5e, races give you:

- **Ability score increases** (e.g., +2 DEX)
- **Racial traits** (darkvision, poison resistance, extra cantrips, etc.)
- **Size and speed** (most are Medium size, 30 ft. walking speed)
- **Languages** (Common + usually one more)

> **Note on newer rules:** The 2024 revised Player's Handbook lets you assign ability score bonuses freely regardless of race. Ask your DM which version they're using — the original (fixed bonuses per race) or the newer flexible rules.

### The Core Races

| Race | Vibe | Key Traits | Good Fit For |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Human** | Versatile, adaptable, everywhere | +1 to ALL scores (or a feat with variant human) | Literally anything. Variant Human is one of the strongest picks in 5e because you get a feat at level 1. |
| **Elf** | Graceful, long-lived, magical | +2 DEX, darkvision, can't be put to sleep, trance instead of sleep | Ranger, Rogue, Wizard, Fighter |
| **Dwarf** | Tough, stubborn, crafty | +2 CON, darkvision, poison resistance, weapon proficiencies | Fighter, Cleric, Paladin |
| **Halfling** | Small, lucky, brave | +2 DEX, reroll natural 1s (Lucky trait!), brave (advantage vs. fear) | Rogue, Ranger, Bard |
| **Half-Elf** | Best of both worlds | +2 CHA and +1 to two others, darkvision, two extra skill proficiencies | Bard, Warlock, Paladin, Sorcerer |
| **Half-Orc** | Fierce, powerful | +2 STR, +1 CON, extra crit damage, drop to 1 HP instead of 0 once | Barbarian, Fighter, Paladin |
| **Gnome** | Curious, clever, quirky | +2 INT, darkvision, advantage on INT/WIS/CHA saves vs. magic | Wizard, Artificer |
| **Tiefling** | Infernal heritage, outsider | +2 CHA, +1 INT, darkvision, fire resistance, free spells | Warlock, Sorcerer, Bard, Paladin |
| **Dragonborn** | Draconic ancestry, proud | +2 STR, +1 CHA, breath weapon (elemental damage in a cone/line) | Paladin, Fighter, Sorcerer |

### Subraces

Most races have **subraces** that further specialize:
- **Elf:** High Elf (free cantrip), Wood Elf (faster, better stealth), Drow/Dark Elf (superior darkvision, innate spells)
- **Dwarf:** Hill Dwarf (extra HP), Mountain Dwarf (armor proficiency, +2 STR)
- **Halfling:** Lightfoot (better hiding), Stout (poison resistance)
- **Gnome:** Forest (minor illusion cantrip), Rock (tinkering)

### Exotic Races (If Your DM Allows)

These appear in expansion books. You might have seen them in BG3:
- **Githyanki** — Astral warriors, great with weapons and psionic powers
- **Aasimar** — Celestial-touched, healing powers, angelic transformation
- **Goliath** — Big, tough mountain folk, can shrug off damage
- **Tabaxi** — Cat people, super fast, climb speed
- **Firbolg** — Gentle forest giants, can turn invisible
- **Kenku** — Flightless bird folk who mimic sounds

There are dozens more. If something speaks to you, ask your DM if it's available.

### How to Choose a Race

For your first character, don't overthink it. Pick based on:

1. **What sounds cool or fun to you?** This matters most.
2. **Does it fit the class you want?** A +2 in your class's primary stat is helpful but not mandatory.
3. **Do you like the racial traits?** Darkvision is incredibly handy. Halfling luck is amazing. These things come up every session.

## Part 2: Classes

Your class is the biggest mechanical choice you make. It determines:
- Your **hit points** (how tough you are)
- Your **abilities** in and out of combat
- Your **proficiencies** (weapons, armor, skills, saves)
- How you **grow** as you level up

### The 5e Classes at a Glance

I've organized these by playstyle. The "complexity" rating is for the player, not the character — some classes require tracking more resources and making more decisions.

---

### Martial Classes (Weapon-Focused)

#### Fighter
- **The BG3 comparison:** Lae'zel
- **What you do:** Hit things. A lot. You're the most versatile martial class — swords, bows, heavy armor, shields, whatever you want.
- **Key feature:** Action Surge (take a whole extra turn once per short rest), Extra Attacks
- **Primary stat:** STR or DEX (your choice — heavy armor brawler or nimble duelist)
- **Complexity:** ★☆☆☆☆ — The simplest class. Great for learning the game.
- **Best for:** "I want to be effective in combat without tracking a bunch of resources."

#### Barbarian
- **The BG3 comparison:** Karlach
- **What you do:** Rage. While raging, you take half damage from physical attacks, deal extra damage, and have advantage on STR checks. You're the party's tank.
- **Key feature:** Rage (limited uses per day), Reckless Attack (advantage on all your attacks, but enemies get advantage on you too)
- **Primary stat:** STR (with good CON)
- **Complexity:** ★☆☆☆☆ — Simple. Rage, hit, repeat.
- **Best for:** "I want to charge in and be unkillable."

#### Monk
- **What you do:** Martial arts. You punch, kick, and move with supernatural speed. No armor needed — your AC comes from WIS + DEX.
- **Key feature:** Ki points fuel abilities like Flurry of Blows (extra attacks), Step of the Wind (dash/disengage as bonus action), Stunning Strike
- **Primary stat:** DEX and WIS
- **Complexity:** ★★★☆☆ — Managing Ki points requires some planning.
- **Best for:** "I want to be fast and punchy with a mystical flavor."

#### Rogue
- **The BG3 comparison:** Astarion
- **What you do:** Sneak, stab, steal, and be incredibly skilled. Your Sneak Attack adds massive damage to one attack per turn when you have advantage or an ally nearby.
- **Key feature:** Sneak Attack, Expertise (double proficiency in chosen skills), Cunning Action (hide, dash, or disengage as a bonus action every turn)
- **Primary stat:** DEX
- **Complexity:** ★★☆☆☆ — Simple in combat, lots of out-of-combat utility.
- **Best for:** "I want to be stealthy and deadly, with tons of skill versatility."

---

### Spellcasting Classes (Magic-Focused)

#### Wizard
- **The BG3 comparison:** Gale
- **What you do:** You have the biggest spell list in the game. Fireball, Counterspell, Shield, Fly — the iconic spells are yours. Fragile but incredibly powerful.
- **Key feature:** Spellbook (you can learn new spells from scrolls), Arcane Recovery (get spell slots back on a short rest)
- **Primary stat:** INT
- **Complexity:** ★★★★★ — The most spells to choose from. Preparation and spell choice matter a lot.
- **Best for:** "I want ultimate magical versatility and don't mind being squishy."

#### Sorcerer
- **What you do:** Cast spells through innate power (not study). Fewer spells known than a Wizard, but you can bend magic with Metamagic — twin a spell, cast it subtly, extend its range.
- **Key feature:** Metamagic, Sorcery Points (fuel for Metamagic and converting spell slots)
- **Primary stat:** CHA
- **Complexity:** ★★★☆☆ — Fewer choices than Wizard, but Metamagic adds a layer.
- **Best for:** "I want to be a natural-born spellcaster who bends the rules of magic."

#### Warlock
- **The BG3 comparison:** Wyll
- **What you do:** You made a pact with a powerful being (demon, fey, eldritch entity) for magic. You have very few spell slots (1-4), but they ALL recharge on a short rest, and Eldritch Blast is the best damage cantrip in the game.
- **Key feature:** Eldritch Blast + Agonizing Blast invocation (reliable, customizable damage), Pact Boon (special features from your patron)
- **Primary stat:** CHA
- **Complexity:** ★★☆☆☆ — Fewer slots = fewer decisions. Eldritch Blast carries you.
- **Best for:** "I want dark/mysterious magic without managing a huge spell list."

---

### Hybrid Classes (Weapons + Magic)

#### Paladin
- **What you do:** Holy warrior. Heavy armor, big weapon, powerful smites. You can dump spell slots into your melee attacks for massive burst damage (Divine Smite).
- **Key feature:** Divine Smite (spend a spell slot when you hit to add 2d8+ radiant damage — devastating on crits), Lay on Hands (pool of healing), Aura of Protection (you and nearby allies add your CHA to all saves)
- **Primary stat:** STR and CHA
- **Complexity:** ★★☆☆☆ — Straightforward. "Do I smite or save the slot?"
- **Best for:** "I want to be a tanky warrior who can also heal and obliterate evil."

#### Ranger
- **The BG3 comparison:** Minsc (loosely)
- **What you do:** Wilderness expert with martial skills and some nature magic. Good with bows, can have an animal companion (Beast Master subclass), and excels in exploration.
- **Key feature:** Fighting Style, spellcasting (Hunter's Mark is your bread and butter), Favored Enemy, Natural Explorer
- **Primary stat:** DEX (and some WIS)
- **Complexity:** ★★☆☆☆
- **Best for:** "I want to be an archer/tracker with some magic."

#### Cleric
- **The BG3 comparison:** Shadowheart
- **What you do:** Channel divine power from a god. Clerics are the most versatile class in 5e — depending on your subclass (Domain), you can be a healer, a tank in heavy armor, a damage dealer, or a battlefield controller.
- **Key feature:** Healing, Channel Divinity (powerful domain-specific abilities), full spellcasting, prepare spells daily from the entire Cleric list
- **Primary stat:** WIS
- **Complexity:** ★★★☆☆ — Choosing prepared spells daily requires some game knowledge.
- **Best for:** "I want to support the party AND be a powerhouse."

#### Bard
- **What you do:** Music, charm, and jack-of-all-trades. You're the ultimate skill monkey — good at everything, with support spells, healing, and control magic. You buff allies with Bardic Inspiration.
- **Key feature:** Bardic Inspiration (give allies bonus dice to add to rolls), Jack of All Trades (add half proficiency to every skill you're NOT proficient in), full spellcasting
- **Primary stat:** CHA
- **Complexity:** ★★★☆☆
- **Best for:** "I want to be the party's face, support allies, and be useful in every situation."

#### Druid
- **What you do:** Nature magic and shapeshifting. Wild Shape lets you turn into animals — a bear for combat, a spider for scouting, an eagle for travel. Full spellcasting with great control and healing spells.
- **Key feature:** Wild Shape, full nature spell list (Entangle, Call Lightning, Healing Word)
- **Primary stat:** WIS
- **Complexity:** ★★★★☆ — Wild Shape adds a whole extra set of decisions and stat blocks.
- **Best for:** "I want to be a shapeshifter with powerful nature magic."

---

### The Oddball

#### Artificer
- **What you do:** Magical inventor. You infuse items with magic, build gadgets, and support the party with utility. Think fantasy engineer.
- **Primary stat:** INT
- **Complexity:** ★★★☆☆
- **Note:** This is from the Eberron/Tasha's books, not the original Player's Handbook. Ask your DM if it's available.

## First-Timer Recommendations

If you want **simplicity + effectiveness**, pick one of these:

| If You Want To... | Play a... | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Hit things and be tough | **Fighter** | Simple, flexible, always useful. Champion subclass is zero-complexity. |
| Hit things and be angry | **Barbarian** | Rage is intuitive, you're hard to kill, and it's fun. |
| Sneak and do massive damage | **Rogue** | Sneak Attack is satisfying, and you'll dominate out-of-combat skill checks. |
| Smite evil and feel powerful | **Paladin** | Big damage, good defense, some healing. Very satisfying. |
| Blast things with dark magic | **Warlock** | Easy to manage, Eldritch Blast carries combat, great flavor. |
| Heal and support (and still fight) | **Cleric (Life Domain)** | The classic healer, but in heavy armor with a mace. Not fragile at all. |

## What To Decide Next

At this point, you don't need to lock anything in. But start thinking about:

1. **What fantasy archetype excites you?** The brooding rogue? The raging barbarian? The holy knight? The trickster bard?
2. **Do you want to cast spells?** If yes, how much? (A little = Paladin/Ranger. A lot = Wizard/Cleric/Druid.)
3. **Do you like being the face of the group** (talking to NPCs) or the **muscle** (solving problems with force)?
