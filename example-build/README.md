# Example Build: The Ninja Rogue of Kara-Tur

> **This is an example of a fully realized D&D 5e character, built using the guides in this repository.** It demonstrates how race, class, background, lore, and backstory come together into a cohesive concept. The character presented here — Fusetsu, an Assassin Rogue from the monasteries of Kara-Tur — is meant to be inspiring, not prescriptive. Use it as a reference point for building your own deeply realized character.

---

## Ninjas in the Forgotten Realms: They Exist

The Forgotten Realms isn't just the Sword Coast. The world is called **Toril**, and on the same planet, far to the east, lies a continent called **Kara-Tur** — the Forgotten Realms' equivalent of East and Southeast Asia. It's been official D&D lore since the 1980s.

Kara-Tur includes:

| Region | Real-World Inspiration | Key Flavor |
|---|---|---|
| **Shou Lung** | Imperial China | Largest empire on Toril. Bureaucracy, martial arts traditions, secret societies, philosophical monasteries. |
| **Kozakura** | Feudal Japan | Samurai, ninja clans, honor culture, island nation. |
| **T'u Lung** | Southern China / Southeast Asia | Shou Lung's southern rival. Political intrigue, jungle terrain. |
| **Wa** | Isolationist Japan | Strictly traditional, closed borders, warrior monks. |
| **Tabot** | Tibet | Mountain monasteries, spiritual discipline. |
| **Koryo** | Korea | Martial traditions, scholarly culture. |

### The Shou Connection to the Sword Coast

Here's the beautiful part: **the Shou are already on the Sword Coast.** There's a Shou community in Baldur's Gate, Waterdeep, and other major cities. Shou merchants, scholars, and — yes — martial artists have made the journey west. This means a Kara-Tur character doesn't need an exotic explanation for being in a Sword Coast adventure. Shou people are a recognized, if uncommon, presence.

> In BG3, NPCs with East Asian features or names represent the Shou diaspora.

## The Monastic Assassin Tradition

Established Forgotten Realms lore offers several organizations that fit the "monastic assassin" archetype. Below are real FR factions, plus a custom concept created for this build:

### Real Factions to Draw From

**The Shadow Thieves of Amn**
The largest thieves' guild in Faerûn (featured prominently in Baldur's Gate II). Highly organized, with cells of specialists and assassins among their ranks. Not monastic, but very much "trained professional."

**The Monks of the Dark Moon**
An order devoted to **Shar**, goddess of darkness, loss, and secrets. They blend monastic discipline with shadow magic. Secretive, militant, and dangerous. Very ninja-coded.

**The Shadowmasters of Telflamm**
An assassin guild in the far east of Faerûn (near Kara-Tur's borders). They blend eastern martial traditions with western guild structure.

**The Long Death**
A monastic order obsessed with understanding death itself. They study killing as a philosophical discipline. Cold, methodical, terrifying.

### A Custom Concept: The Silent Cord (話さぬ糸)

*This is a homebrew concept — the kind of thing a player would propose to a DM, who can approve, tweak, or weave it into the world.*

**The Silent Cord** is a secretive order based in the mountain monasteries between Shou Lung and Tabot. They are not assassins for hire — they are **correctors**. When the balance of power tips toward tyranny, when corruption poisons a court, when a warlord threatens the peace of a province, the Silent Cord sends one of its trained agents to *cut the thread*.

Members train from childhood in:
- **Physical discipline** — acrobatics, blade work, pressure-point combat
- **Mental discipline** — meditation, observation, patience (this is where the WIS focus shines in the fiction)
- **Infiltration** — disguise, social reading, silent movement
- **Philosophy** — they are not murderers. Each action is weighed. "A single precise cut prevents a thousand wounds."

The order teaches that **wisdom is the true weapon** — the blade is merely its extension. This is the in-world justification for prioritizing WIS as a Rogue. The character isn't just dexterous; they're *perceptive, patient, and deliberate*. They read a room before entering it. They know where the guards are before the guards know they exist.

**Why the Sword Coast?** That's the hook for the backstory. Some possibilities:
- Sent on a mission that took them west — and something went wrong.
- Completed training but left the order. Why?
- The order was destroyed. One of the last survivors.
- On a long-term assignment: observe and report on western power structures.

## How This Maps to Game Mechanics

### Race: Shou Human

Mechanically, this is a **Human** (the Shou are human — it's an ethnicity, not a separate race).

**Variant Human** is the strongest pick:
- +1 to two ability scores (DEX and WIS)
- One extra skill proficiency
- **One feat at level 1** — this is huge

Good starting feats for this build:
- **Alert** — +5 to initiative, can't be surprised. Very ninja. Always acts first.
- **Observant** — +5 to passive Perception and Investigation, +1 WIS. The "I notice everything" character.
- **Skulker** — Hide when lightly obscured, missed ranged attacks don't reveal position. Literal ninja stealth.

Alternatively, a **Wood Elf** works beautifully (DEX bonus, bonus to stealth in nature, faster movement, and the long-lived elf combined with Shou cultural training makes an interesting story).

### Class: Rogue

**Subclass options** (chosen at level 3):

| Subclass | Vibe | Key Feature |
|---|---|---|
| **Assassin** | The classic ninja | Advantage + auto-crit on surprised enemies. Proficiency with disguise and poisoner's kits. Devastating first round. |
| **Swashbuckler** | Duelist / skirmisher | Sneak Attack when 1-on-1, free disengage from anyone attacked, CHA-based initiative bonus. More mobile. |
| **Shadow** (from the Monk class, for reference) | — | For players considering a multiclass into Monk later, Way of Shadow is literally the ninja subclass. |

**Assassin** fits this concept best. That first-round burst of auto-crit Sneak Attack on a surprised enemy is peak ninja fantasy.

### Stat Priority

| Priority | Stat | Why |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | **DEX** | Everything — attacks, AC, stealth, initiative |
| 2nd | **WIS** | Perception, Insight, Survival. The character's training in awareness and discipline. |
| 3rd | **CON** | Hit points. Not tanky, but not made of paper. |
| 4th | **INT** | Investigation, Arcana. The analytical mind. |
| 5th | **CHA** | Not the party face — the quiet professional. |
| 6th | **STR** | Dump stat. Finesse over brute force. |

### Skills

Rogues get 4 skill proficiencies (most in the game). Good picks for this concept:
- **Stealth** (DEX) — Non-negotiable for a ninja.
- **Perception** (WIS) — Sees everything. Feeds into the WIS investment.
- **Insight** (WIS) — Reads people. Another WIS skill.
- **Acrobatics** (DEX) — Rooftop movement, flips, balance. The martial artist physicality.

The background provides 2 more skills. The **Urchin** or **Criminal** background adds Sleight of Hand and Deception. This build uses **Far Traveler** for Survival and Investigation, which fits the story better.

## What to Discuss With the DM

Before finalizing any character like this, run these things by the DM:

1. **"Is Kara-Tur part of the world?"** — In Forgotten Realms, the answer is almost certainly yes. In homebrew settings, there may be an equivalent.
2. **"Is the Silent Cord okay, or should it be adjusted?"** — Good DMs appreciate when players bring backstory hooks. They may want to tie it to something already planned.
3. **"Are we starting at level 1?"** — This matters for when the subclass is chosen (level 3).
4. **"Can I use Variant Human?"** — Some DMs restrict it because the free feat is very strong.

## Character Summary

> **Concept:** A trained operative from an eastern monastic order, now on the Sword Coast. Quiet, observant, precise. Fights with twin blades and kills before the enemy knows they're there.
>
> **Race:** Variant Human (Shou ethnicity)
> **Class:** Rogue -> Assassin subclass at level 3
> **Stats:** DEX > WIS > CON > INT > CHA > STR
> **Fighting style:** Dual wielding (shortswords — finesse and light)
> **Personality:** Patient, disciplined, few words. Watches before acting. Respects skill and dedication in others.

The WIS focus isn't "optimal" in a min-max sense, but it makes the character *feel* different from every other Rogue at the table — not a street thief, but a trained specialist with preternatural awareness.
