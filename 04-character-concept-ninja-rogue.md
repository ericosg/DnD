# Character Concept — The Ninja Rogue of Kara-Tur

## Ninjas in the Forgotten Realms: They Exist

The Forgotten Realms isn't just the Sword Coast. The world is called **Toril**, and on the same planet, far to the east, lies a continent called **Kara-Tur** — the Forgotten Realms' equivalent of East and Southeast Asia. It's been official D&D lore since the 1980s.

Kara-Tur includes:

| Region | Real-World Inspiration | Key Flavor |
|---|---|---|
| **Shou Lung** | Imperial China | Largest empire on Toril. Bureaucracy, martial arts traditions, secret societies, philosophical monasteries. |
| **Kozakura** | Feudal Japan | Samurai, ninja clans, honor culture, island nation. |
| **T'u Lung** | Southern China / Southeast Asia | Shou Lung's southern rival. Political intrigue, jungle terrain. |
| **Wa** | Isolationist Japan | Strictly traditional, closed borders, warrior monks. |
| **Tabot** | Tibet | Mountain monasteries, spiritual discipline. |
| **Koryo** | Korea | Martial traditions, scholarly culture. |

### The Shou Connection to the Sword Coast

Here's the beautiful part: **the Shou are already on the Sword Coast.** There's a Shou community in Baldur's Gate, Waterdeep, and other major cities. Shou merchants, scholars, and — yes — martial artists have made the journey west. This means your character doesn't need an exotic explanation for being in a Sword Coast adventure. Shou people are a recognized, if uncommon, presence.

> In BG3, you may have noticed NPCs with East Asian features or names. That's the Shou diaspora.

## The Monastic Assassin Tradition

Here's where established lore meets creative backstory. There are several organizations in Forgotten Realms lore that fit the "monastic assassin" archetype. Below are real FR factions, plus a custom concept for your character:

### Real Factions You Could Draw From

**The Shadow Thieves of Amn**
The largest thieves' guild in Faerûn (you encounter them in Baldur's Gate II). Highly organized, cells of specialists, assassins among their ranks. Not monastic, but very "trained professional."

**The Monks of the Dark Moon**
An order devoted to **Shar**, goddess of darkness, loss, and secrets. They blend monastic discipline with shadow magic. Secretive, militant, and dangerous. Very ninja-coded.

**The Shadowmasters of Telflamm**
An assassin guild in the far east of Faerûn (near Kara-Tur's borders). They blend eastern martial traditions with western guild structure.

**The Long Death**
A monastic order obsessed with understanding death itself. They study killing as a philosophical discipline. Cold, methodical, terrifying.

### A Custom Concept: The Silent Cord (話さぬ糸)

*This is a homebrew concept — something you'd propose to your DM, who can approve, tweak, or weave it into their world.*

**The Silent Cord** is a secretive order based in the mountain monasteries between Shou Lung and Tabot. They are not assassins for hire — they are **correctors**. When the balance of power tips toward tyranny, when corruption poisons a court, when a warlord threatens the peace of a province, the Silent Cord sends one of its trained agents to *cut the thread*.

Members train from childhood in:
- **Physical discipline** — acrobatics, blade work, pressure-point combat
- **Mental discipline** — meditation, observation, patience (this is where your WIS focus shines in the fiction)
- **Infiltration** — disguise, social reading, silent movement
- **Philosophy** — they are not murderers. Each action is weighed. "A single precise cut prevents a thousand wounds."

The order teaches that **wisdom is the true weapon** — the blade is merely its extension. This is your in-world justification for prioritizing WIS as a Rogue. You're not just dexterous; you're *perceptive, patient, and deliberate*. You read a room before you enter it. You know where the guards are before they know you exist.

**Why you're on the Sword Coast:** That's the hook for your backstory. Some possibilities:
- You were sent on a mission that took you west — and something went wrong.
- You completed your training but left the order. Why?
- The order was destroyed. You're one of the last.
- You're on a long-term assignment: observe and report on western power structures.

## How This Maps to Game Mechanics

### Race: Shou Human

Mechanically, you'd be a **Human** (the Shou are human — it's an ethnicity, not a separate race).

**Variant Human** is the strongest pick:
- +1 to two ability scores (DEX and WIS)
- One extra skill proficiency
- **One feat at level 1** — this is huge

Good starting feats for this build:
- **Alert** — +5 to initiative, can't be surprised. Very ninja. You always act first.
- **Observant** — +5 to passive Perception and Investigation, +1 WIS. The "I notice everything" character.
- **Skulker** — You can hide when lightly obscured, missed ranged attacks don't reveal you. Literal ninja stealth.

Alternatively, if you don't want to be human, a **Wood Elf** works beautifully (DEX bonus, bonus to stealth in nature, faster movement, and the long-lived elf + Shou cultural training combo is an interesting story).

### Class: Rogue

**Subclass options** (chosen at level 3):

| Subclass | Vibe | Key Feature |
|---|---|---|
| **Assassin** | The classic ninja | Advantage + auto-crit on surprised enemies. Proficiency with disguise and poisoner's kits. Devastating first round. |
| **Swashbuckler** | Duelist / skirmisher | Sneak Attack when 1-on-1, free disengage from anyone you attack, CHA-based initiative bonus. More mobile. |
| **Shadow** (from the Monk class, for reference) | — | If you ever wanted to multiclass into Monk later, Way of Shadow is literally the ninja subclass. |

**Assassin** fits your concept best. That first-round burst of auto-crit Sneak Attack on a surprised enemy is peak ninja fantasy.

### Stat Priority

| Priority | Stat | Why |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | **DEX** | Everything you do — attacks, AC, stealth, initiative |
| 2nd | **WIS** | Perception, Insight, Survival. Your character's training in awareness and discipline. |
| 3rd | **CON** | Hit points. You're not tanky, but you don't want to be made of paper. |
| 4th | **INT** | Investigation, Arcana. The analytical mind. |
| 5th | **CHA** | You're not the party face — you're the quiet professional. |
| 6th | **STR** | Dump stat. You use finesse, not brute force. |

### Skills You'd Want

Rogues get 4 skill proficiencies (most in the game). Good picks for this concept:
- **Stealth** (DEX) — Non-negotiable. You're a ninja.
- **Perception** (WIS) — You see everything. Feeds into your WIS investment.
- **Insight** (WIS) — You read people. Another WIS skill.
- **Acrobatics** (DEX) — Rooftop movement, flips, balance. The martial artist physicality.

Your background will give you 2 more skills. If you take the **Urchin** or **Criminal** background, you could add Sleight of Hand and Deception.

## What to Discuss With Your DM

Before finalizing, run these things by your DM:

1. **"Is Kara-Tur part of your world?"** — If they're running Forgotten Realms, the answer is almost certainly yes. If they're running homebrew, they may have their own equivalent.
2. **"Is the Silent Cord okay, or would you like to adjust it?"** — Good DMs love when players bring backstory hooks. They may want to tie it to something they've already planned.
3. **"Are we starting at level 1?"** — This matters for when you pick your subclass (level 3).
4. **"Can I use Variant Human?"** — Some DMs restrict it because the free feat is very strong.

## Character Summary So Far

> **Concept:** A trained operative from an eastern monastic order, now on the Sword Coast. Quiet, observant, precise. Fights with twin blades and kills before the enemy knows they're there.
>
> **Race:** Variant Human (Shou ethnicity) or Wood Elf
> **Class:** Rogue → Assassin subclass at level 3
> **Stats:** DEX > WIS > CON > INT > CHA > STR
> **Fighting style:** Dual wielding (shortswords or scimitars — both are finesse)
> **Personality:** Patient, disciplined, few words. Watches before acting. Respects skill and dedication in others.

This is shaping up to be a really memorable character. The WIS focus isn't "optimal" in a min-max sense, but it makes your character *feel* different from every other Rogue at the table — you're not a street thief, you're a trained specialist with preternatural awareness.
