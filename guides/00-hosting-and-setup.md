# Hosting D&D at Your Place — Practical Setup Guide

## The Table Situation

### What Works Best
A **dining table or large desk** where everyone sits in chairs. Why:
- Everyone needs space to roll dice, reference their character sheet, and maybe take notes.
- The DM typically needs the *most* space — they may have a DM screen, books, notes, maps, and minis spread out.
- You need to be able to see each other's faces and hear each other clearly.

### Can You Use a Couch + Coffee Table?
**It works, but it's not ideal.** Here's why:
- Coffee tables are low — leaning forward to roll dice or read a map for 3-4 hours gets uncomfortable.
- Couches make it hard to have your character sheet, dice, and a drink all within reach.
- The DM will struggle with limited surface area.

**If the coffee table is your only option**, it's totally fine for a first session — people have played D&D on floors, in cars, and at picnic tables. But if you have a dining table or can borrow a folding table, that's the move.

### The DM's Space
Your DM will likely want:
- Room for a **DM screen** (a tri-fold cardboard screen that hides their notes/rolls) — about 2 feet wide
- Space for at least one book (the Monster Manual or adventure module)
- Room for their own dice and notes
- **Ask your DM** if they need anything specific — some DMs are low-tech (notebook + dice), others bring laptops and tablets

## What You Need to Bring / Have

### The Essentials (Must Have)
| Item | Notes |
|---|---|
| **Your character sheet** | Built during character creation (Session Zero or beforehand). Paper or printed. |
| **A set of dice** | d4, d6, d8, d10, d12, d20, and a percentile d10. A standard set costs ~$5-10. |
| **A pencil + eraser** | Not a pen. Your HP, spell slots, and inventory change constantly. |
| **Something to take notes** | A small notebook or scrap paper. Jot down NPC names, quest details, etc. |

### Nice to Have (But Not Required)
| Item | Notes |
|---|---|
| **A miniature or token** | A little figure to represent your character on a map. A coin, chess piece, or LEGO figure works perfectly. |
| **Extra d6s** | Some abilities use multiple d6s (like Sneak Attack or Fireball). 4-6 extra d6s are handy. |
| **Spell cards or printed spell list** | If you play a spellcaster. |
| **A phone/tablet** | For looking up rules on D&D Beyond (dndbeyond.com). Many groups use digital character sheets there. |

### What You Do NOT Need to Buy
- **Miniatures / figurines** — The DM handles this if the group uses them. Many groups just use tokens, coins, or candy (yes, really).
- **Battle maps / play mats** — DM's responsibility. Some DMs use them, many don't.
- **Any books** — The Basic Rules are free online. Your DM has the expensive books.
- **A DM screen** — That's the DM's thing.

## Hosting Tips

### Food & Drinks
- D&D sessions typically run **3-5 hours**. Have snacks and drinks available.
- Keep food away from character sheets and books (grease + paper = sadness).
- A separate snack table or side surface helps a lot.

### The Space
- **Lighting:** Make sure everyone can read their sheets. Dim mood lighting is cool but not if people are squinting.
- **Noise:** D&D requires a lot of listening and talking. Turn off the TV. Background music is fine and can be great for atmosphere (your DM may handle this).
- **Seating for everyone:** Count your players + DM. Make sure everyone has a seat at the table.
- **Power outlets:** If anyone uses a laptop or tablet, having a charger nearby helps for long sessions.

### Before the Session
Ask your DM these logistics questions:
- [ ] How many players total? (so you know how many seats/space you need)
- [ ] How long will the session run?
- [ ] Should we do character creation together at the table, or come with characters ready?
- [ ] Do you (the DM) need anything specific for your setup?
- [ ] Are we using physical maps/minis or theater of the mind (no map, just imagination)?

---

## Quick Summary

> **Ideal setup:** Dining table + chairs, good lighting, snacks on the side.
> **You need:** Character sheet, dice, pencil, notebook.
> **The DM handles:** Maps, minis, books, the screen.
> **If coffee table is all you have:** It'll work! Just be ready to shift around.
