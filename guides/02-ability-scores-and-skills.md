# Ability Scores & Skills — The Foundation of Your Character

## The Six Ability Scores

Every creature in D&D has six stats. If you've played Baldur's Gate 3, these are identical — they work exactly the same way on tabletop.

| Ability | What It Represents | High Score Means... | Low Score Means... |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Strength (STR)** | Raw physical power | You hit hard, carry more, kick down doors | You struggle with heavy things |
| **Dexterity (DEX)** | Agility, reflexes, balance | You're fast, sneaky, hard to hit | You're clumsy, slow to react |
| **Constitution (CON)** | Toughness, endurance, health | You have more HP, resist poison, endure pain | You're frail, get sick easily |
| **Intelligence (INT)** | Memory, logic, learning | You know things, solve puzzles, study magic | You're not book-smart |
| **Wisdom (WIS)** | Perception, intuition, common sense | You notice things, read people, resist tricks | You're oblivious, easily deceived |
| **Charisma (CHA)** | Force of personality | You persuade, intimidate, lead, cast with flair | You're awkward, unpersuasive |

---

## How Scores Become Modifiers

Your raw score (like 16) doesn't directly get added to rolls. Instead, it produces a **modifier** — and the modifier is what you actually use.

| Score | Modifier |
|---|---|
| 1 | -5 |
| 2-3 | -4 |
| 4-5 | -3 |
| 6-7 | -2 |
| 8-9 | -1 |
| **10-11** | **0** |
| 12-13 | +1 |
| 14-15 | +2 |
| 16-17 | +3 |
| 18-19 | +4 |
| 20 | +5 |

**The formula:** (Score - 10) / 2, rounded down.

- A score of **10 is average** for a commoner.
- Player characters typically range from **8 to 16** at level 1, occasionally reaching 17-18.

> **Example:** A character with 16 Strength has a +3 modifier. When they swing a sword, they roll d20 + 3 (plus their proficiency bonus — more on that in a moment).

## Skills — Specific Applications of Your Abilities

Each ability has **skills** tied to it. Skills represent trained areas of expertise. Here's the full list:

### Strength
- **Athletics** — Climbing, swimming, jumping, grappling

### Dexterity
- **Acrobatics** — Backflips, balance, tumbling
- **Sleight of Hand** — Pickpocketing, hiding objects, tricks
- **Stealth** — Sneaking, hiding

### Intelligence
- **Arcana** — Knowledge of magic, spells, magical items
- **History** — Knowledge of past events, wars, civilizations
- **Investigation** — Deduction, searching for clues, examining details
- **Nature** — Knowledge of plants, animals, terrain, weather
- **Religion** — Knowledge of gods, rituals, holy symbols

### Wisdom
- **Animal Handling** — Calming, controlling, or reading animals
- **Insight** — Reading body language, detecting lies, sensing motives
- **Medicine** — Stabilizing the dying, diagnosing illness
- **Perception** — Noticing things — hearing footsteps, spotting traps, seeing hidden doors
- **Survival** — Tracking, foraging, navigating wilderness

### Charisma
- **Deception** — Lying convincingly, disguising intent
- **Intimidation** — Threatening, scaring, coercing
- **Performance** — Acting, music, storytelling, entertaining
- **Persuasion** — Convincing through logic, charm, or diplomacy

### How Skill Checks Work

1. DM says: *"Make a Wisdom (Perception) check."*
2. You roll a d20.
3. You add your **Wisdom modifier**.
4. If you are **proficient** in Perception, you also add your **proficiency bonus**.

That's it.

## Proficiency Bonus

This is a flat bonus that represents your general level of training. It starts at **+2** at level 1 and slowly increases as you level up.

| Level | Proficiency Bonus |
|---|---|
| 1-4 | +2 |
| 5-8 | +3 |
| 9-12 | +4 |
| 13-16 | +5 |
| 17-20 | +6 |

You add your proficiency bonus to:
- **Skills you're proficient in** (chosen during character creation)
- **Saving throws you're proficient in** (determined by your class)
- **Attack rolls with weapons you're proficient in**
- **Spell attack rolls** (if you're a caster)

### What "Proficient" Means in Practice

Everyone can *attempt* most skills. The difference:
- **Not proficient in Stealth:** d20 + DEX modifier only
- **Proficient in Stealth:** d20 + DEX modifier + proficiency bonus

So at level 1 with 16 DEX:
- Not proficient: d20 + 3 = anywhere from 4 to 23
- Proficient: d20 + 3 + 2 = anywhere from 6 to 25

That +2 matters more than it looks.

## Saving Throws — Resisting Bad Things

Saving throws are like skill checks, but you don't choose to make them — they happen *to* you.

- A dragon breathes fire --> **Dexterity saving throw** (dodge the flames)
- A wizard casts Hold Person --> **Wisdom saving throw** (resist the mind control)
- You eat poisoned food --> **Constitution saving throw** (tough it out)

Each class is proficient in **two** saving throw types. For example:
- Fighters: Strength & Constitution
- Rogues: Dexterity & Intelligence
- Wizards: Intelligence & Wisdom

## Advantage & Disadvantage

If you've played BG3, you already know this — it works the same way.

- **Advantage:** Roll 2d20, take the **higher** result. (Good circumstances.)
- **Disadvantage:** Roll 2d20, take the **lower** result. (Bad circumstances.)

They cancel each other out — if you have both, you roll normally.

This replaces most situational modifiers from older editions. Instead of "+2 because you're on higher ground, -1 because it's raining," the DM just says "you have advantage" or "you have disadvantage." It keeps things fast.

## Passive Scores

Your DM will sometimes use **passive checks** — a flat number that represents your baseline alertness. The most common one:

**Passive Perception = 10 + Perception modifier**

This is what the DM checks against when deciding if you *notice* something without actively looking. If a hidden enemy needs to sneak past you, they roll Stealth against your passive Perception.

> You don't roll for passive checks. They're always "on."

## How This Connects to Character Creation

When you build your character, you'll:

1. **Generate six ability scores** (by rolling dice or using a point-buy system)
2. **Assign them** to STR, DEX, CON, INT, WIS, CHA based on what your class needs
3. **Pick skill proficiencies** from a list your class and background offer
4. Your **race** may give +1 or +2 to certain scores (or you can distribute freely with newer rules)

All of this happens step by step in the character creation guide. For now, just understand:
- **High score = better at that thing**
- **Modifier = what you add to rolls**
- **Proficiency = extra bonus in things you're trained in**
